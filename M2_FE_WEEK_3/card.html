BEAR

Bears are large mammals found in forests and mountains around the world. They are omnivores, eating both plants and animals.

LION

Lions are powerful big cats that live in groups called prides. They are known as the "king of the jungle" for their strength and dominance.

SHARK

Sharks are ancient fish with sharp teeth and excellent senses for hunting. They play an important role in keeping ocean ecosystems balanced.

PENGUINS

Penguins are fascinating birds that cannot fly but are excellent swimmers. They live mainly in the Southern Hemisphere, especially in Antarctica, and are well adapted to cold environments. Their black and white feathers provide camouflage in the ocean, protecting them from predators.

ELEPHANTS

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. They are known for their long trunks, large ears, and thick gray skin. Elephants use their trunks for many tasks, such as eating, drinking, and communicating with other elephants. They live in herds, usually led by an older female called a matriarch. Elephants are herbivores, feeding on grass, fruits, and tree bark. They are intelligent and social animals, capable of showing emotions like joy, grief, and compassion.

RHINOCEROSES

Rhinoceroses, or rhinos, are large herbivorous mammals known for their thick skin and one or two horns on their noses. They live in parts of Africa and Asia, usually in grasslands and savannas. Rhinos eat mostly plants, such as grass, leaves, and fruits. Despite their size and strength, many species of rhinos are endangered because of illegal hunting and habitat loss. These animals play an important role in their ecosystems by shaping the land and helping plants grow.
